Google Ads vs Meta Ads: Which Is Better for Indian Small Businesses?

← Back to Blog
By PageOne·7 min read·June 30, 2026·Yelahanka, Bangalore

Should you spend your ad budget on Google Ads or Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)? For most Indian small businesses, the answer depends on one thing: whether people are already searching for what you sell.

Quick answer: Use Google Ads when people actively search for your product or service (high intent). Use Meta Ads when you need to create demand, show visuals, or reach people who don't know you yet. Many businesses get the best results running both.

When Google Ads Wins

Google Ads shows your business to people actively searching for what you offer. If someone types "AC repair Bangalore" or "wedding photographer Yelahanka", they have intent — they want to buy now.

  • Best for: services, local businesses, B2B, high-intent products
  • Buyer is already looking — you just need to appear
  • Typically higher cost-per-click, but higher conversion rate

When Meta Ads Wins

Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram) show your business to people based on their interests and behaviour — not what they searched. It's about creating demand.

  • Best for: e-commerce, fashion, food, lifestyle, visual products
  • Great for building brand awareness and retargeting
  • Lower cost-per-click, strong for impulse and discovery

Cost Comparison in India

Google AdsMeta Ads
Typical CPC (India)Rs. 8–50+Rs. 2–15
Buyer intentHigh (searching)Low–medium (browsing)
Best forServices, B2B, localE-commerce, visual brands

Which Should Your Business Use?

  • Local service business (salon, repair, clinic) → start with Google Ads
  • Online store / product brand → start with Meta Ads
  • B2B / lead generation → Google Ads, then add Meta for retargeting

The Honest Answer: Run Both

The most effective strategy is usually a combination. Google Ads captures people ready to buy now, while Meta Ads builds awareness and brings back people who visited but didn't convert. Start with whichever fits your business, prove ROI, then expand.
